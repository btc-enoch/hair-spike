# 03 — Competitive Landscape

Researched 2026-06-05. This is **not** a blue ocean — "AI hairstyle changer" is
crowded — but the specific combination we're targeting is barely served.

## Established players

- **Perfect Corp / YouCam** — the incumbent. Real-time AR hair *colour* try-on +
  AI style generation (bangs, volume, extensions). Powers many brand/salon apps
  via SDK.
- **ModiFace (L'Oréal)** — AR hair colour, white-labelled into brand apps
  ("Style My Hair"). Polished, colour-focused.
- **FaceApp / Fotor / ImagineArt / Cutout.pro / Krea** — photo-in → AI-styled
  photo-out. Single still image.

## Emerging — video / multi-angle (our lane)

- **Style Orbit AI** — "360° video hairstyle simulator." **Closest direct
  competitor.** But it's *upload front/side/back stills* and *synthesises* the
  motion — not a live captured rotation, and not voice-driven.
- **HairHunt** — 100+ styles with 360° video preview.
- **The Right Hairstyles** — added a rotating front/side preview (2024); stitched
  still views, not a true captured turn.
- **Mootion / insMind** — apply hair styles/colours onto an existing video.

## The white space

Nobody cleanly combines all three of our pillars:

| Capability | Incumbents | **Us** |
|---|---|---|
| Input | upload a still (or a few) | **capture a live 5s rotation of the real head** |
| Output | synthesised/imagined angles | grounded in the user's **actual multi-view capture** |
| Control | tap a gallery / type a prompt | **voice-first** |
| Styles | often library/templated | **generative** |

Two genuinely unclaimed differentiators:
1. **Voice-first interaction** — found *zero* competitors leading with "speak the
   style you want."
2. **Captured-rotation as input** (not synthesised) + **generative** styles.

## Honest risk

The tech combo is the wedge, but it's not a permanent moat: Perfect Corp could add
a capture+voice mode in a quarter if it proves out. Durable advantage would come
from **experience quality** and a **specific audience** (e.g. salon consults, or a
particular demographic), not the tech alone.
